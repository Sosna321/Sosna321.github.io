Ph.D. Student in Mathematics · Howard University

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mathematics at Howard University with a passion for applying quantitative and computational methods to address public health challenges in under-resourced communities. My research focuses on developing mathematical and statistical models for infectious diseases—with a particular emphasis on HIV drug resistance dynamics. I am committed to leveraging advanced modeling and machine learning techniques to promote health equity and improve patient outcomes globally.

About Me

My academic interests lie at the intersection of mathematics, epidemiology, and public health. I am particularly driven by the potential of mathematical modeling to illuminate disease dynamics, guide clinical decision-making, and support public health interventions. Beyond my research, I am passionate about using data-driven approaches to address disparities in healthcare access and outcomes.

At Howard University, I am actively involved in research projects that combine differential equations, numerical analysis, and statistical modeling to explore the complex mechanisms that drive infectious disease spread. My long-term goal is to contribute to the development of predictive models and decision-support tools that can improve health systems in underserved regions around the world.
